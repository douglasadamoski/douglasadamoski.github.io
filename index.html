Douglas Adamoski

Postdoctoral Researcher
Max Planck Institute for Heart and Lung Research · Bad Nauheim, Germany

Structural and molecular biologist working at the intersection of biochemistry, cell biology, and evolutionary genetics. My research explores enzyme regulation through protein filamentation, metabolic reprogramming in cancer, and genetic compensation via transcriptional adaptation in mitochondrial biology.

Research Interests

🧬

Protein Filamentation & Enzyme Regulation

Investigating how metabolic enzymes such as glutaminase (GLS) and cystathionine beta-synthase (CBS) form regulatory filaments, using cryo-EM and functional assays.

🔬

Cancer Metabolism

Exploring how cancer cells exploit glutamine and glucose pathways, and how non-coding RNAs (e.g. CCAT2) rewire metabolic networks to support tumor growth.

🫀

Mitochondrial Biology & Transcriptional Adaptation

Current postdoctoral work: genetic compensation through transcriptional adaptation in nuclear genes governing mitochondrial morphology.

🧫

Structural & Functional Genomics

Integrating genomics, transcriptomics, metabolomics, and structural biology to connect molecular architecture to cellular phenotypes.

☀️

UV Biology & Photobiology

Characterizing differential cellular effects of far-UVC (222 nm) and near-UVC (254 nm) irradiation, with implications for safe disinfection technologies.

📊

RNA Quantification & Reproducibility

Developing best practices for accurate and reproducible quantification of coding and non-coding RNAs in profiling and sequencing studies.
